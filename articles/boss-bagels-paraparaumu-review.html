The Verdict

The Boss Stacker earned a perfect 10/10 from me. Yes, it's greasy‚Äîbut that's entirely the point. Sometimes you don't want refined; you want real, satisfying food that doesn't apologize for being indulgent. At $18, it's at the premium end of their menu, but the portion size justifies the price‚Äîthis is easily a meal that'll keep you satisfied for hours.

The Boss BLT scored a respectable 9.5/10, with the only criticism being that it could have benefited from some haloumi to elevate it from great to exceptional. It's a minor quibble, though‚Äîthe bagel was still delicious as served and well-priced at $14.

In an era of $18+ caf√© mains and $6 coffee becoming the norm, Boss Bagels hits a sweet spot. Most bagels sit in the $10-15 range, which feels fair for the generous portions and quality ingredients. The daily deals are particularly clever‚Äî$10 for bagels that would normally cost $14-15 represents genuine value, not just marketing spin.

What Boss Bagels lacks in seating, it makes up for in quality and convenience. The drive-through model is perfect for grabbing breakfast on the way to the beach, picking up lunch for a park picnic, or simply avoiding the faff of restaurant service when you're short on time.

The portion sizes are generous‚Äîthese aren't delicate caf√© bagels, they're proper, substantial meals. The ingredients taste fresh, and there's a genuine commitment to doing American bagel culture justice.
