The platform leverages cutting-edge adaptive learning algorithms that identify knowledge gaps and personalize question delivery, ensuring students spend time on areas where they need the most work. Scaling Fast, Thinking Big

Despite being in its early stages, KiwiMed is already generating buzz among medical students across New Zealand's two medical schools. The founders report strong early interest, with beta testers praising the platform's intuitive interface and clinically relevant content.

The company is bootstrapped but thinking big. Plans include expanding beyond basic sciences and clinical knowledge to cover the Prescribing Safety Assessment (PSA), OSCE preparation modules, and eventually, resources for junior doctors preparing for vocational training exams.

"We're starting with what we know ‚Äî final year medical student needs ‚Äî but the vision extends far beyond that," the team shared. "We want KiwiMed to be the go-to educational resource for every stage of a New Zealand doctor's training journey."

KiwiMed represents a broader trend in medical education: students taking control of their learning tools. Frustrated by expensive international subscriptions that don't quite fit their needs, these three Wellington students embody the entrepreneurial spirit that's increasingly common in modern medicine.

With New Zealand's medical education landscape rapidly evolving and increasing emphasis on local clinical competency, KiwiMed's timing couldn't be better. The platform addresses a genuine need while showcasing the innovation emerging from New Zealand's medical schools.
