Wellington Phoenix Fight Back to Earn Hard-Fought Draw in Perth

Wellington Phoenix displayed resilience and character to claw back from a two-goal deficit and secure a 2-2 draw against Perth Glory at HBF Park on Saturday night, in front of 7,868 spectators.

First Half: Glory's Fast Start

Perth Glory came out firing in front of their home crowd, taking an early lead through Jaiden Kucharski in the 16th minute. The home side's aggressive start caught the Phoenix off-guard, and they doubled their advantage just ten minutes later when experienced striker Adam Taggart found the net in the 26th minute.

Despite trailing 2-0, the statistics told a different story. Wellington dominated possession throughout the opening period, though they struggled to convert their territorial advantage into clear-cut chances. Perth's game plan was evident ‚Äì defend deep, stay compact, and hit on the counter-attack.

Second Half: Phoenix Rising

Whatever was said in the Wellington dressing room at halftime clearly had an effect. The visitors emerged with renewed purpose and were rewarded just three minutes into the second half. Ifeanyi Eze pulled one back in the 48th minute, giving the Phoenix a lifeline and shifting the momentum decisively in their favour.

Wellington continued to press, controlling 70.8% of possession over the course of the match ‚Äì a remarkable statistic that highlighted their dominance in open play. The pressure finally told in the 69th minute when Carlo Armiento struck to level the scores at 2-2, sending the travelling Phoenix faithful into raptures.

The match statistics paint a clear picture of Wellington's superiority in most facets of play. The Phoenix completed significantly more successful dribbles (12 to Perth's 5), won more duels overall (54.3% success rate compared to Perth's 44.8%), and dominated aerial battles with a 55.2% success rate.

Perth's gameplan of soaking up pressure and hitting on the break worked perfectly in the first half but became increasingly difficult to sustain as the match wore on. They conceded 18 fouls to Wellington's 8 ‚Äì a telling statistic that revealed the defensive pressure they were under for extended periods.

Surprisingly, despite their possession dominance, Wellington managed only one corner to Perth's three, suggesting that while they controlled the ball, breaking down a well-organised Glory defence proved challenging.

What It Means

The draw sees Wellington Phoenix pick up their first point of the A-League season, currently sitting fourth in the ladder. Perth Glory also earned their first point, positioned fifth. Both teams will feel they could have taken all three points ‚Äì Perth for leading 2-0, and Wellington for their overwhelming second-half dominance.

For Wellington coach, the response after going two goals down will be encouraging, particularly the composure shown in maintaining their passing game under pressure. However, the defensive vulnerabilities exposed in the first half will be a concern ahead of their next fixture against Brisbane Roar on 26 October.

Perth Glory, meanwhile, will rue their inability to protect a two-goal lead, though defending with less than 30% possession for large periods was always going to be unsustainable against quality opposition.
