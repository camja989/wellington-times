Wellington Launches Asia-Pacific AI Research Center

By Dr. James Liu • • 7 min read

In a major boost for New Zealand's technology sector, Wellington has been chosen as the location for a new $50 million artificial intelligence research center, in partnership with Victoria University and leading tech companies. The center will focus on ethical AI development, machine learning applications in climate science, and natural language processing.

The facility, located in the city's Innovation Precinct, will house 100 researchers and provide collaboration spaces for industry partners. "Wellington's combination of world-class research institutions, supportive government policies, and growing tech ecosystem makes it the ideal location for this center," said Professor Linda Zhang, the center's incoming director.

Initial research projects will include developing AI systems for earthquake prediction, climate modeling, and healthcare diagnostics. The center is expected to create 200 high-skilled jobs and attract international talent to the region.
