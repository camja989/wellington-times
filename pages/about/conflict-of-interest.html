Conflict of Interest Policy

Our Commitment to Transparency

At The Wellington Times, we are committed to maintaining the highest standards of journalistic integrity and transparency. This policy outlines how we identify, disclose, and manage potential conflicts of interest that may affect our reporting.

Core Principle: When our staff have personal, financial, or professional connections to the subjects we cover, we believe in full transparency rather than silence. Our readers deserve to know about any potential conflicts so they can make informed judgments about our coverage.

What Constitutes a Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest exists when a journalist or The Wellington Times has:

  • Financial interests (ownership, investment, or compensation) in organizations or topics being covered
  • Personal relationships with subjects of coverage (family, close friends, romantic relationships)
  • Professional affiliations or memberships that could influence reporting
  • Direct involvement in the events, organizations, or communities being covered
  • Pending employment opportunities or business relationships with subjects of coverage

Disclosure Requirements

When a potential conflict of interest exists, we will:

  • Prominently disclose the conflict within the article itself, typically near the beginning
  • Explain the nature and extent of the relationship or interest
  • Describe any steps taken to ensure objectivity in reporting
  • Include information about additional sources or verification used to maintain balance
Example Disclosure:
"Disclosure: [Author Name], [role] of The Wellington Times, is currently a [relationship to subject]. This article is based on publicly available data and interviews with multiple [relevant experts/stakeholders]."

Editorial Independence

The Wellington Times maintains editorial independence through:

  • Multiple Sources: Articles with potential conflicts include diverse perspectives and expert opinions beyond the author's experience
  • Fact-Based Reporting: All claims are verified through public records, official data, or expert sources
  • Clear Attribution: Personal experiences and opinions are clearly labeled as such and distinguished from factual reporting
  • Editorial Review: Content with disclosed conflicts receives additional scrutiny to ensure fairness and accuracy

Advertising and Sponsored Content

We clearly distinguish between editorial content and advertising:

  • All sponsored content is clearly labeled as "SPONSORED" or "ADVERTISEMENT"
  • We do not accept payment for editorial coverage
  • Advertising relationships do not influence editorial decisions
  • We do not cover businesses or organizations in which we have financial interests without full disclosure

Personal Business Interests

Wellington Times staff are prohibited from:

  • Using non-public information gained through journalism for personal financial benefit
  • Trading in securities or investments related to their coverage areas
  • Accepting gifts, travel, or services from sources that could influence coverage
  • Writing about their own business ventures without prominent disclosure and editorial oversight

Community Involvement

As a small, independent news organization deeply embedded in Wellington's community, our staff may have connections to local organizations, institutions, and issues. When these connections are relevant to our coverage, we disclose them to maintain transparency.

Reader Trust

Your trust is our most valuable asset. If you believe we have failed to disclose a relevant conflict of interest, or if you have concerns about our coverage, please contact us at:

  • Email: thewgtimes@gmail.com
  • Our contact form

Ongoing Commitment

This policy is a living document. As The Wellington Times grows and evolves, we will continue to refine our approach to conflicts of interest, always prioritizing transparency and reader trust.

Questions? If you have questions about this policy or about specific disclosures in our articles, we encourage you to reach out. Transparency works both ways, and we welcome dialogue with our readers.
