Corrections Policy

The Wellington Times is committed to accuracy and accountability in all our journalism. We take our responsibility to readers seriously and strive to correct errors promptly and transparently.

Our Commitment to Accuracy

We aim to be accurate in all our reporting and to avoid mistakes. However, we recognize that errors can occur despite our best efforts. When they do, we are committed to correcting them quickly, clearly, and transparently.

How We Handle Corrections

Minor Corrections

Minor errors such as spelling mistakes, grammatical errors, or factual inaccuracies that do not materially affect the story's meaning will be corrected in the article with a note at the bottom indicating when the correction was made.

Significant Corrections

Significant errors that materially affect the story's accuracy or meaning will be corrected with a prominent correction notice at the top of the article, including:

  • A clear description of what was incorrect
  • The correct information
  • The date and time the correction was made
  • An explanation of the error, when appropriate

Retractions

In rare cases where an article contains fundamental errors that cannot be adequately corrected, we will retract the article with a full explanation of the reasons for the retraction.

Fact-Checking Process

Before publication, we verify information through:

  • Checking facts against reliable sources
  • Verifying quotes and attributions
  • Seeking comment from relevant parties when appropriate
  • Reviewing statistical and numerical data
  • Cross-referencing claims with authoritative sources

Editorial Standards

Our editorial standards include:

  • Accuracy: All facts are verified before publication
  • Fairness: We present multiple perspectives and seek comment from all relevant parties
  • Independence: We maintain editorial independence from commercial and political interests
  • Transparency: We clearly distinguish between news, analysis, and opinion
  • Accountability: We take responsibility for our reporting and correct errors promptly

How to Report an Error

If you believe we have made an error in our reporting, please contact us:

Email: thewgtimes@gmail.com

Contact Form: Contact Us

Please include:

  • The article URL or headline
  • A clear description of the error
  • Supporting evidence or documentation if available
  • Your contact information so we can follow up if needed

Response Timeline

We aim to:

  • Acknowledge receipt of correction requests within 24 hours
  • Investigate and respond to substantive concerns within 48-72 hours
  • Correct confirmed errors as quickly as possible, typically within 24 hours

Opinion & Commentary

While opinion pieces and commentary represent the views of their authors and may contain subjective analysis, factual claims within opinion pieces are subject to the same correction policy as news articles.

Reader Feedback

We value reader feedback and engagement. Not all reader concerns will result in corrections, but we carefully consider all feedback about our reporting and use it to improve our journalism.

Our Promise to Readers

Accuracy is fundamental to credible journalism. The Wellington Times is committed to getting the facts right, acknowledging when we get them wrong, and maintaining the trust of our readers through transparent and accountable reporting practices.
