About The Wellington Times

The Wellington Times is an independent digital news publication serving Wellington, New Zealand with comprehensive coverage of local and national news, politics, business, technology, sports, and culture.

Our Mission

Founded in 2025, The Wellington Times was established with a clear purpose: to provide Wellington and New Zealand with a truly independent voice in an increasingly consolidated media landscape. While major media conglomerates dominate traditional news coverage, we believe local communities deserve journalism that is free from corporate interests and editorial constraints imposed by large media organizations.

Our mission is to deliver accurate, balanced, and engaging journalism that serves the public interest. We are committed to editorial independence, transparency, and accountability in all our reporting.

Leadership

The Wellington Times is led by Jack Campbell, a Wellington-based entrepreneur and final-year medical student at the University of Otago's Wellington campus. Drawing on his deep connection to the capital city and understanding of community needs, Jack founded The Wellington Times to create a sustainable model for independent journalism in New Zealand.

With a background that spans both healthcare and digital media, Jack brings a unique perspective to news publishing—combining rigorous fact-checking standards with innovative digital storytelling approaches. His vision is to build a news platform that serves readers first, rather than shareholders or corporate interests.

Why Independent Media Matters

New Zealand's media landscape has undergone significant consolidation in recent years. While we respect the important work done by established media organizations, we believe there is a critical need for diverse, independent voices that can offer alternative perspectives and hold all institutions—including media itself—accountable.

The Wellington Times operates outside the traditional media ownership structures, allowing us to:

  • Report on stories without concern for corporate interests or advertiser pressure
  • Provide diverse viewpoints that may not fit conventional media narratives
  • Invest resources in long-form investigative journalism
  • Experiment with new approaches to storytelling and community engagement
  • Maintain full editorial independence in all our coverage

Our Coverage

The Wellington Times provides comprehensive coverage across multiple beats:

  • Politics: In-depth coverage of Parliament, local government, and policy analysis
  • Business & Economics: Wellington's commercial sector, startups, and economic trends
  • Technology: Our thriving tech industry and digital innovation
  • Sports: From the Wellington Phoenix to local community sports
  • Culture: Arts, entertainment, and the creative industries that define our city
  • Opinion: Diverse perspectives on issues that matter to Wellingtonians

Our Standards

The Wellington Times adheres to the highest standards of journalism:

  • Accuracy: We verify all facts and sources before publication
  • Fairness: We present multiple perspectives and seek comment from all relevant parties
  • Transparency: We clearly identify news, analysis, and opinion
  • Corrections: We promptly correct errors and clearly note all amendments
  • Ethics: We follow professional journalism ethics in all our reporting

Support Independent Journalism

The Wellington Times is funded through a combination of advertising revenue, sponsorships, and reader support. We are committed to maintaining a sustainable business model that allows us to invest in quality journalism while preserving our editorial independence.

By choosing to read The Wellington Times and supporting our advertisers, you're helping to sustain independent media in Wellington and New Zealand. Together, we can ensure that diverse, independent voices continue to thrive in our media ecosystem.
