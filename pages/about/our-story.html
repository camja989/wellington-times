Our Story

Founded in 2025, The Wellington Times represents a new chapter in Wellington's rich journalistic tradition.

A New Voice for Wellington

The Wellington Times was born from a simple belief: that our vibrant capital city deserves comprehensive, independent journalism that reflects the diversity, creativity, and dynamism of our community. In an era of rapidly changing media landscapes, we saw an opportunity to create something different—a digital-first publication that combines the integrity of traditional journalism with the innovation of modern storytelling.

Our Foundation

Wellington has always been a city of ideas, a place where culture, politics, technology, and enterprise converge in exciting ways. From the halls of Parliament to the studios of Miramar, from the bustling waterfront to the creative suburbs spreading across our hills, our city pulses with stories waiting to be told.

We launched The Wellington Times to ensure these stories reach the people who need to hear them—whether that's breaking news from the Beehive, insights into our thriving tech sector, coverage of the Phoenix's latest match, or reviews of the newest exhibition at Te Papa.

Our Commitment

From day one, we've been committed to:

  • Independence: We're free from political affiliations and corporate interests
  • Accuracy: Every fact is checked, every source is verified
  • Transparency: We own our mistakes and correct them promptly
  • Community: We serve Wellington first and foremost
  • Innovation: We embrace new ways to tell stories and engage readers

Looking Forward

As we grow, our mission remains unchanged: to inform, engage, and serve the people of Wellington. We're building more than a news site—we're building a platform for community dialogue, a resource for civic engagement, and a record of our city's ongoing story.

Whether you're a long-time Wellingtonian or new to our windy city, whether you're interested in politics, sports, culture, or all of the above, The Wellington Times is here for you.

"We believe in the power of good journalism to strengthen communities, hold power to account, and celebrate the stories that make Wellington the extraordinary place it is."

— The Editorial Team
