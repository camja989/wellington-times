Parliament Passes Landmark Climate Change Legislation

By Sarah Mitchell ‚Ä¢ ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

In a historic session lasting well into the night, New Zealand's Parliament has passed comprehensive climate change legislation that sets ambitious targets for carbon neutrality by 2050. The bill, which received cross-party support, establishes a framework for reducing greenhouse gas emissions and transitioning to renewable energy sources.

Prime Minister addressed the House, stating: "This legislation represents a commitment to future generations. We are taking decisive action to address one of the greatest challenges of our time." The new law includes provisions for regular emissions reviews, accountability measures for industries, and incentives for green technology adoption.
